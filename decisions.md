- **2026-06-02 — Output model: letter grades → number + tier word + verdict + description (supersedes both the custom-band letter mapping AND the academic-scale idea).** We considered letters, considered just reusing the standard academic A–F scale, and deliberately chose **number + descriptor word + expandable explanation** because explanation-over-grade is the project's core thesis — the thing ESPN's Trade Machine can't do. The letter (B+/C+/…) carried academic baggage that fought the domain meaning of the score (a "B+" implies "good student"; a 77 here means "good rebuild haul for *this* team's goal"). New contract carries four things, with a critical distinction held: **score** (number, unchanged — 77) and **tier** (mechanical word from the score band) are cheap lookups; **verdict** (one-line, trade-specific) and **description** (paragraph, trade-specific) are *generated prose* the real scorer must produce. Tier bands (single source of truth in `src/domain/grades.js` via `tierForScore` + the `TIERS` constant): 85–100 Franchise Win / 75–84 Strong / 70–74 Solid / 55–69 Lateral / 40–54 Negative / 0–39 Damaging. The bottom of the scale is now spread across three distinct words (Lateral / Negative / Damaging) instead of compressed into C/D/F. Ripple: dropped `letter` from the domain grade factories and the console formatter (now renders "90 · Franchise Win"); relabeled the rubric doc's band table + example ladder (no score changes); replaced the stub's `overallGrade`/`band` with `tier` (computed via `tierForScore(77)` so the stub mirrors what the real scorer does) + the hardcoded `verdict`/`description` prose. Frontend now renders three depth layers: headline "77 · Strong" (neutral — no good/bad color, score is goal-relative), the component bars + formula (unchanged), and a collapsed-by-default `VerdictDetails` disclosure ("What this trade does") revealing the one-liner then the paragraph. ⚠️ Vocabulary flagged for reconsideration: **Strong (75–84) vs Solid (70–74) are near-synonyms** and a reader may not intuit which ranks higher; word→band mapping is in one place (`TIERS`) so it's a one-line change. Also: the spec mapped the ~50 example to "Lateral," but `tierForScore(50)` is **Negative** (40–54) — labeled it Negative in the doc to keep the doc consistent with the code, and flagged.
